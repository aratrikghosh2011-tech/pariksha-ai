RESPONSE: Step 1: Use Newton's second law: Force = mass x acceleration
Step 2: acceleration = Force / mass = 20 / 5 = 4 m/s^2
Step 3: Use v = u + at where u = 0, a = 4 m/s^2, t = 4 s
Step 4: v = 0 + 4 x 4 = 16 m/s
Answer: Acceleration = 4 m/s^2, Velocity after 4 seconds = 16 m/s

Answer: Current = 2 A, Voltage across 6 ohm = 12 V, Voltage across 3 ohm = 6 V

INSTRUCTION: A radioactive element has a half-life of 5 years. How much of a 100 g sample will remain after 20 years?
RESPONSE: Step 1: Number of half-lives in 20 years = 20 / 5 = 4 half-lives
Step 2: After 1st half-life: 100 / 2 = 50 g
Step 3: After 2nd half-life: 50 / 2 = 25 g; after 3rd: 25 / 2 = 12.5 g; after 4th: 12.5 / 2 = 6.25 g
Step 4: Alternatively, remaining mass = initial mass x (1/2)^n = 100 x (1/2)^4 = 100/16 = 6.25 g
Answer: Mass remaining after 20 years = 6.25 g

INSTRUCTION: A radioactive substance has a half-life of 8 days. After how many days will (7/8) of the original sample have decayed?
RESPONSE: Step 1: If (7/8) has decayed, then (1/8) of the original mass remains
Step 2: Remaining fraction = (1/2)^n where n = number of half-lives
Step 3: (1/2)^n = 1/8 = (1/2)^3, so n = 3 half-lives
Step 4: Time = n x half-life = 3 x 8 = 24 days
Answer: Time for 7/8 of the sample to decay = 24 days

INSTRUCTION: A radioactive nucleus has atomic number 92 and mass number 238. It emits one alpha particle and two beta particles successively. Find the atomic number and mass number of the resulting nucleus.
RESPONSE: Step 1: Alpha decay: mass number decreases by 4, atomic number decreases by 2
Step 2: After alpha emission: mass number = 238 - 4 = 234, atomic number = 92 - 2 = 90
Step 3: Beta decay: mass number unchanged, atomic number increases by 1 (for each beta particle)
Step 4: After 2 beta emissions: mass number = 234, atomic number = 90 + 2 = 92
Answer: Resulting nucleus has mass number = 234 and atomic number = 92

INSTRUCTION: A sample of radioactive material has an initial activity and reduces to (1/4) of its original activity in 40 years. Calculate the half-life of the substance.
RESPONSE: Step 1: Activity reduces to 1/4 means the quantity has reduced to 1/4 of original
Step 2: (1/2)^n = 1/4 = (1/2)^2, so n = 2 half-lives have passed
Step 3: Total time = n x half-life, so 40 = 2 x half-life
Step 4: Half-life = 40 / 2 = 20 years
Answer: Half-life of the substance = 20 years
