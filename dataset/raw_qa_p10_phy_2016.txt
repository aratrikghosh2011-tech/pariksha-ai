Question 1(a)
(i) Give an example of a non-contact force which is always of attractive nature.

(ii) How does the magnitude of this non-contact force on the two bodies depend on the distance of separation between them? [2]

Answer

(i) A non-contact force which is always of attractive nature is gravitational force.

(ii) Magnitude of non-contact forces on the two bodies is inversely proportional to the square of the distance of separation between them.

Question 4(e)
Which coil of a step up transformer is made thicker and why? [2]

Answer

Primary Coil is made thicker than the secondary coil in a step up transformer. As current in primary coil is more than in the secondary coil so the wire in the primary coil is kept thicker.
The use of thicker wire in a coil reduces it's resistance and therefore, reduces the loss of energy as heat in that coil.

Question 6(a)
(i) What is the principle of method of mixtures?

(ii) What is the other name given to it?

(iii) Name the law on which the principle is based. [3]

Answer

(i) According to principle of mixtures, heat lost by a hot body is always equal to the heat gained by a cold body provided no heat is lost to the surroundings.

(ii) The principle of method of mixtures is also called the principle of calorimetry.

(iii) It is based on the law of conservation of energy.

Question 8(a)

(iii) Why are the waves mentioned by you not audible to us? [3]

Answer

(i) The waves used for echo depth sounding are ultrasonic waves.

(ii) They can travel undeviated through long distances.

(iii) Ultrasonic waves are not audible to us because they are of frequency above 20 kHz and the audible frequency range is 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz. Hence, we cannot hear ultrasonic waves.

Question 8(b)
(i) What is an echo?

(ii) State two conditions for an echo to take place. [3]

Answer

(i) The sound heard after reflection from a distinct obstacle (such as a cliff, a hillside etc.) after the original sound has ceased is called an echo.

(ii) Conditions for an echo to take place are :

The size of the reflector must be large enough as compared to the wavelength of the sound wave.
The intensity of sound should be such that the reflected sound reaching the ear is sufficiently loud to be audible.

Question 10(a)
Arrange α, β and γ rays in ascending order with respect to their

(i) Penetrating power.

(I) Increasing order of penetrating power of α, β and γ rays is α<β<γ.
